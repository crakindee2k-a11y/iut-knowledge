CTX QA-verifier lever KILLED: synthetic ROC-AUC 0.9515 looked strong but fails the real decision test

Strategy verified HIGH

Why it mattersThis closes off what looked like the single biggest lever for pushing past 0.9 (ceiling math said fixing these errors could add 5-9 points on the CTX band alone). Knowing it's dead -- and WHY -- stops anyone from re-spending GPU time rebuilding the same judge-feature approach.

CTX band was reopened because ceiling math showed its 13.6% error rate is now the binding constraint for a 0.9+ target (perfect NULL judge + frozen lexical CTX still caps global at 0.939). Error dissection on the 132 labeled CTX rows found the 18 errors split into two families: ~9 surface-mismatch FN (Bengali numeral-word vs digit, tokenization) and ~9-10 wrong-entity-answer FP, ALL sitting at token-overlap ov=1.0 (response is verbatim IN the context but answers the wrong sub-question, e.g. 1st-vs-last principal, Philosopher's Stone vs Chamber of Secrets). Naive ceiling math (perfectly fixing just those FP) projects macro-F1 up to 0.9365-0.943 on the 132-row sample. Built ctx_verifier_eval.py: applies the EXACT Qwen3-32B judge prompt from kernel_repro_0829_improved.py as a second-stage check, ONLY on CTX rows with token_recall>=0.85 (719/1361 test rows), asking 'does response correctly answer question given context' instead of trusting the lexical rule blindly. Validated on a 1000-row balanced sample built from BenHalluEval's 594 host-generated high-overlap hallucinations (synthetic wrong-entity swaps) + matching faithful rows, using the real Qwen3-32B thinking judge on Kaggle GPU. Result: ROC-AUC=0.9515; confusion at best threshold 0.96: tp445 fp56 tn444 fn55 (in-sample F1 0.889) -- looks like a strong signal. BUT the deployment-relevant test is different: on the real CTX distribution, ~88% of high-overlap rows are TRUE faithful, so any hard veto must have very high precision or it destroys more faithful predictions than hallucinations it catches. Sweeping every threshold, NO threshold achieves <=2% or <=5% faithful false-drop; only the <=10%-drop threshold works, and even that only catches 85.6% of FP while dropping 49/500 faithful. Root cause: the judge is confidently WRONG on ~11% of correct answers (P near 0 on truly faithful rows) -- a 'Trust Me I'm Wrong' failure mode that doesn't move with the threshold. Lexical overlap is blind at ov=1.0 for both the true-halluc and the confidently-misjudged-faithful rows, so a single monotonic LogReg weight on the judge's P score cannot separate them (that would need a non-linear/region model). Projected onto the real ~900-row high-overlap test band, a hard-flag veto catches ~90 real hallucinations but breaks ~87 real faithful rows (1.02:1 catch/break) = net macro-F1 neutral-to-negative. Separately flagged: BenHalluEval's 594 synthetic cases are host-generated systematic entity-swaps, a DIFFERENT and likely easier distribution than our organic FP (only n=9) -- so a good synthetic AUC would have been an UNTRUSTWORTHY positive requiring further organic + domain-shift validation anyway; the ACTUAL gate failure is a trustworthy NEGATIVE that ends the lane outright.
How we knowReal Qwen3-32B judge run on 1000 rows: ROC-AUC 0.9515, but the highest-precision usable threshold only reaches a 10% faithful-false-drop rate, not the <=5% needed to net-positive on given the real ~88% faithful in the high-overlap band.
Do thisDo not build a CTX judge/QA-verifier feature. Keep SKIP_CTX_JUDGE=True + lexical-only for the CTX band in the locked kernel. Reopening this lane would require a non-linear/region-based combiner that can isolate where the judge's confident-wrong mass lives, not a single LogReg weight on judge-P -- unbuilt, unproven, and the underlying problem (judge confidently misjudging 11% of correct high-overlap answers) may not be fixable by combiner design at all.

The 0.9515 AUC number is on BenHalluEval's SYNTHETIC high-overlap hallucinations (host-generated systematic entity swaps), which may be an easier pattern than our organic test errors (only n=9 known cases). That means the negative result is the trustworthy direction here (if the judge can't hit a usable precision even on the easy synthetic case, it's not going to work on the harder organic one) -- but a hypothetically GOOD synthetic result would NOT have been enough to ship without further organic + domain-shift checks, per the small-fold-trap lesson.

DE @deen When the AI judge gets a Bangladesh-knowledge question wrong, it's not unsure -- it's confidently, completely wrong. That rules out the easy fixes (better thresholds, asking the model to double-check itself) and points at a real knowledge gap instead. 🔥 hot read verified redact F-024 v1

NULL band errors are confidently wrong, not uncertain (39/44 on n=169) -- self-answer and cross-lingual self-consistency levers both killed

Strategy verified MEDIUM

Why it mattersThis explains why several NULL-band levers (self-answer, cross-lingual) failed for the SAME underlying reason, and stops the team from re-trying variations on the same dead idea. It also tells us where the effort should go instead: sourcing outside knowledge, not squeezing more out of the model's own parametric memory.

From a fully-judged 169-row NULL checkpoint (thinking-ON, real Qwen3-32B judge P scores): raw-judge =0.736 at threshold 0.55, with 44/169 errors (17 FP + 27 FN). Critical finding: 39/44 errors have |P-0.5|>0.4, meaning the model is SURE but WRONG -- only 5/44 are genuinely near the 0.5 uncertain boundary. This rules out calibration/threshold tuning as a lever (it can only touch those 5 rows) and marks the errors as a KNOWLEDGE/REASONING gap, not a confidence-calibration gap. Error rate by question type: IDIOM 46% (6/13), DEFINITION 43% (6/14), WORLDFACT 25% (28/114), NUMERIC/DATE 14% (4/28) -- idiom+definition are only 16% of rows but a disproportionate share of errors, though at n=13/14 this sub-slice itself is a small-fold read. Of the 28 worldfact errors specifically, 17 are false-negatives where the judge flags a genuinely CORRECT Bangladesh-specific answer as hallucinated (e.g. Mujib born in Tungipara, constitution date 16 Dec 1972) purely because it doesn't recognize the fact -- the organizer-warned 'skeptical false-flag on unfamiliar-but-true facts' pattern. Two levers were built specifically to attack this confident-wrong class and both were killed on design-critique: (1) SAME-LANGUAGE SELF-ANSWER -- have the model answer its own prompt from parametric knowledge, then compare via embedding similarity to the given response. Killed because it queries the SAME parametric knowledge the verdict-judge already used: on the dominant confident-wrong case the model believes a wrong fact, so its self-answer is wrong in the same direction -- anti-correlated with correctness, not just redundant. Also the embedding instrument itself was found broken (BGE-m3 cosine similarity on 2-token Bangla facts put unrelated wrong answers close together and correct paraphrases far apart, AUC~0.5 uninterpretable). (2) CROSS-LINGUAL SELF-CONSISTENCY (a distinct mechanism from the earlier-rejected translate-then-judge approach) -- ask the same factual question in English and Bengali and check agreement. Killed for multiple compounding reasons: only 115/169 NULL rows are even language-neutral candidates (39 are BN-language-intrinsic word-meaning/grammar questions and 15 are math-reasoning, both nonsensical to ask in English); the effective applicable-positive count is roughly 56, giving a Hanley-McNeil AUC standard error of about 0.067 -- too small to distinguish a real 0.62 signal from noise; and 's domain shift toward more Bangladesh-local government/news/legal content makes the technique LESS applicable over time, not more, since BD-local facts are unknowable by the model in any language.
How we know39 of 44 judge errors on the 169-row labeled NULL set are confident mistakes (probability far from 0.5), not uncertain ones -- directly measured from the judge's own output probabilities.
Do thisDo not re-attempt same-language self-answer or cross-lingual self-consistency as NULL-band levers -- both are knowledge-gap-bound, not calibration-fixable. NULL band's real ceiling looks to be near its current ~0.72-0.74 without an EXTERNALLY-sourced knowledge injection (offline RAG, gold-answer lookup, or a more Bangladesh-knowledgeable open model).

n=169 is a small fold -- the team has been burned before by locking decisions on similarly-sized folds that mispredicted at scale (see the TigerLLM 0.764-held-out-to-0.690-LB regression). Treat the taxonomy and error-rate percentages as directional, not exact; the two killed-lever conclusions are on firmer ground since they rest on structural arguments (same knowledge source, language-inapplicability) rather than just the fold's point estimate.
